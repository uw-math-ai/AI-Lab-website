UW Math AI Lab at ICLR 2026

Five papers from UW Math AI Lab researchers were presented at the International Conference on Learning Representations in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil — showcasing world-class work at the frontier of AI for mathematics.

ICML 2026 Spotlight — Top 2.2% Vasily Ilin's work also earned acceptance as a main-conference spotlight paper at ICML 2026, one of the most competitive venues in machine learning.

Five Papers at ICLR 2026

Paper 01  ·  ICLR 2026
Semantic Search over 9 Million Mathematical Theorems
Luke Alexander, Eric Leonen, Sophie Szeto, Artemii Remizov, Ignacio Tejeda, Giovanni Inchiostro, Vasily Ilin, Jarod Alper

Searching for mathematical results remains difficult: most existing tools retrieve entire papers, while mathematicians and theorem-proving agents often seek a specific theorem. We introduce semantic theorem retrieval across a unified corpus of 9.2 million theorem statements — the largest publicly available collection of human-authored, research-level theorems.

OpenReview
Paper 02  ·  ICLR 2026
Learning to Repair Lean Proofs from Compiler Feedback
Evan Wang, Simon Chess, Daniel Lee, Siyuan Ge, Ajit Mallavarapu, Vasily Ilin

As neural theorem provers become increasingly agentic, the ability to interpret and act on compiler feedback is critical. Existing Lean datasets consist almost exclusively of correct proofs, offering little supervision for understanding and repairing failures. We address this gap with a fine-tuned repair model trained on erroneous proofs paired with compiler diagnostics and corrections.

OpenReview
Paper 03  ·  ICLR 2026
CircuitBuilder: From Polynomials to Circuits via Reinforcement Learning
Weikun Zhang, Rohan Pandey, Bhaumik Mehta, Kaijie Jin, Naomi Morato, Archit Ganapule, Michael Ruofan Zeng, Jarod Alper

Motivated by auto-proof generation and Valiant's VP vs. VNP conjecture, we study the problem of discovering efficient arithmetic circuits to compute polynomials using addition and multiplication gates. We formulate this as a single-player game where an RL agent builds the circuit within a fixed number of operations.

OpenReview
Paper 04  ·  ICLR 2026
A Neural Score-Based Method for Deterministic Collisional Plasma Simulation
Vasily Ilin, Jingwei Hu

Plasma modeling is central to the design of nuclear fusion reactors, yet simulating collisional plasma kinetics from first principles remains a formidable computational challenge. The Vlasov-Maxwell-Landau equations couple six-dimensional phase-space transport to self-consistent electromagnetic fields through the nonlinear, nonlocal Landau collision operator. We introduce a neural score-based approach that sidesteps this bottleneck.

OpenReview
Paper 05  ·  ICLR 2026
DiScoFormer: Plug-In Density and Score Estimation with Transformers
Vasily Ilin, Petr Sushko

Estimating probability density and its score from samples remains a core problem in generative modeling, Bayesian inference, and kinetic theory. Existing approaches are split between classical kernel methods that scale poorly with dimension and neural score models that require retraining per distribution. DiScoFormer bridges this gap with a plug-in transformer-based estimator.
